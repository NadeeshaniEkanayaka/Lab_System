OUR DEPARTMENTS

Blood Test

This department is responsible for analyzing blood samples to diagnose conditions such as anemia, leukemia, and blood clotting disorders. Hematology tests include complete blood count (CBC), coagulation studies, and blood smears.

Clinical Chemistry

This department analyzes blood, urine, and other bodily fluids to diagnose and monitor conditions like diabetes, kidney disease, and liver function. Tests in this department include glucose, electrolytes, liver function tests, and renal function tests.

Microbiology

AThis department identifies and tests for infectious agents, such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites, to diagnose infections and guide treatment. Common tests include cultures, sensitivity testing, and rapid antigen/antibody tests.

Immunology

This department assesses the body's immune response by testing for antibodies and antigens, which helps diagnose conditions like autoimmune diseases and infectious diseases. Tests include HIV, hepatitis, autoimmune panels, and allergy testing.

Genomics

This department focuses on analyzing DNA and RNA to diagnose genetic disorders, infectious diseases, and cancer. Tests include polymerase chain reaction (PCR), DNA sequencing, and gene expression analysis.

Cardiology

This department specializes in diagnostic testing related to the cardiovascular system, including the heart and blood vessels. The Cardiology Unit performs a variety of tests to evaluate heart function, diagnose heart conditions, and assess overall cardiovascular health.
